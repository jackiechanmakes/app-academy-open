Here are external resources that helped retrain my brain to feel safe in my body so I could heal:

  • Neuro Orthopaedic Institute Australasia (Noigroup)
    • Experts on pain science.
    • Check out their book, Explain Pain, which demystifies pain. Research shows that the more you understand pain, the less it'll hurt.
  • Body Keeps the Score by Bessel van der Kolk
    • Bessel van der Kolk is a psychiatrist, author, researcher, and educator.
    • His research focuses on how trauma impacts neurobiology, development, and attachment. van der Kolk believes that lasting healing only happens when it is safe for trauma victims to inhabit their bodies, and to tolerate feeling what they feel, and knowing what they know. This may involve trauma processing, neurofeedback, theater, meditation, play, and yoga.
  • Safe Space Meditation
    • I generally struggle with meditation, but safe space meditation resonates with me.
    • Safe space meditation is a stress-reduction technique that can be used to soothe your body by imagining a peaceful, safe place where you can relax.
  • Huberman Lab
    • Andrew Huberman is a neuroscientist and tenured professor in the department of neurobiology and, by courtesy, psychiatry and behavioral sciences at Stanford School of Medicine.
    • Check out his podcast. His research focuses on brain development, brain function, and neural plasticity.
  • r/aww Subreddit
    • A subreddit for cute and cuddly pictures.
    • It is all things that make you go AWW, which works to calm your body's physical responses to stress or anxiety.
